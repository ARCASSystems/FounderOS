## Key Legal Framework

**Primary legislation:**
- Federal Decree-Law No. 33/2021 — UAE Labour Law (effective Feb 2, 2022; replaced 1980 law)
- Cabinet Decision No. 1/2022 — Implementing regulations
- Ministerial Decree No. 46/2022 — Work permits and labour mobility
- Ministerial Decree No. 48/2023 — Emiratisation targets and penalties

**Applies to:** All private sector employees in the UAE.
**Excludes:** DIFC and ADGM employees (separate laws), federal/local government, domestic workers (Decree-Law 9/2022), armed forces/police.

## Employment Contracts

### All Fixed-Term Post-February 2022
New law eliminated "unlimited contract" concept. All contracts are fixed-term (limited), maximum 3 years, renewable. Existing unlimited contracts should have been converted by February 2024. Auto-renewal: if both parties continue after expiry without a new contract, renewed on same terms.

### Required Contents (Article 8)
Every contract must include: employer/employee details, job title and description, start date and duration, probation period (if any, max 6 months), remuneration breakdown (basic + allowances), working hours, rest days, leave entitlements, notice period, termination conditions, place of work.

**Language:** Should be in Arabic (or bilingual). Arabic prevails in disputes. **Registration:** All contracts registered with MOHRE via Tasheel.

### Work Models (Article 7)
Law recognizes: full-time, part-time, temporary, flexible, remote, job-sharing, compressed work week. Each must be reflected in the contract.

## Probation

- Maximum 6 months. Cannot be extended or repeated with same employer.
- **Employer termination during probation:** 14 days' written notice.
- **Employee termination during probation:** 14 days' notice if staying in UAE with new employer; 1 month's notice if leaving UAE.
- If employment continues past probation, period counts toward total service.

## Working Hours and Overtime

- **Standard:** 8 hours/day or 48 hours/week
- **Ramadan:** Reduced by 2 hours/day (all employees)
- **Breaks:** Minimum 1 hour after 5 consecutive hours (not counted as working time)
- **Overtime:** Max 2 additional hours/day. Normal overtime: base + 25%. Between 9PM-4AM: base + 50%.
- **Rest day work:** Base + 50% (or substitute rest day)
- **Rest days:** Minimum 1 per week (typically Friday)
- **Exemptions:** Senior management and positions of trust

## Leave Entitlements

| Type | Entitlement |
|---|---|
| **Annual leave** | 30 calendar days/year (after 1 year); 2 days/month during first year (after 6 months) |
| **Sick leave** | 90 days/year: first 15 full pay, next 30 half pay, remaining 45 unpaid |
| **Maternity** | 60 days: 45 full pay + 15 half pay. Additional 45 days unpaid (medical complications). 2 nursing breaks/day (30 min each) for 6 months. |
| **Paternity** | 5 working days (within 6 months of birth) |
| **Bereavement** | 5 days (spouse); 3 days (parent, child, sibling, grandchild, grandparent) |
| **Study leave** | 10 working days/year (if 2+ years service, UAE-accredited institution) |
| **Hajj leave** | 30 calendar days once during employment (unpaid unless agreed) |

## Remuneration and WPS

### Salary Structure
Typical: basic salary + housing allowance + transport allowance + other allowances. Contract must specify each component separately. **This matters because gratuity is calculated on basic salary only.**

### Wage Protection System (WPS)
All private sector employers must pay salaries through WPS-approved bank transfers by the contractual due date. Non-compliance: fines, suspension of new work permit applications, administrative penalties.

### Deductions
Only permitted for: loan repayment (max 10% per installment), social insurance (UAE/GCC nationals), employee-caused damages (max 5 days' wages per incident, requires investigation), court orders. Total deductions cannot exceed 50% of monthly salary.

## End-of-Service Gratuity

### Eligibility
All employees with 1+ years continuous service. **No gratuity if:** <1 year service, or Article 44 dismissal.

### Formula
- **First 5 years:** 21 calendar days of BASIC salary per year
- **After 5 years:** 30 calendar days of BASIC salary per year
- **Pro-rated** for partial years
- **Cap:** Total cannot exceed 2 years' total remuneration
- **Daily rate:** Monthly basic salary ÷ 30

### Calculation Examples

**3 years, AED 10,000 basic:** 333.33 × 21 × 3 = **AED 21,000**

**7 years, AED 15,000 basic:** First 5: 500 × 21 × 5 = 52,500. Next 2: 500 × 30 × 2 = 30,000. **Total: AED 82,500**

**4 years 8 months, AED 8,000 basic:** 266.67 × 21 × 4.667 = **AED 26,133**

**12 years, AED 25,000 basic:** First 5: 833.33 × 21 × 5 = 87,500. Next 7: 833.33 × 30 × 7 = 175,000. Subtotal: 262,500. Cap check: 2 years' total = 600,000. Under cap. **Total: AED 262,500**

### Key Details
- Based on LAST basic salary (not total package)
- Paid within 14 days of last working day
- Employer can deduct amounts owed (loans, damages)
- Under new law: no reduction for resignation (old-law reductions no longer apply)
- UAE/GCC nationals on GPSSA: employer social insurance contributions replace gratuity

### Common Mistakes
1. Calculating on total salary instead of basic
2. Forgetting to pro-rate partial years
3. Not paying within 14 days
4. Not accounting for 2-year cap
5. Applying old-law resignation reductions to post-2022 contracts

## Termination

### With Notice (Article 43)
- Minimum 30 days, maximum 90 days (per contract). Written notice. Employee continues working and getting paid. 1 paid day off/week during notice to seek new employment. Garden leave allowed (paid, not working).

### Without Notice — Gross Misconduct (Article 44)
Employer may terminate immediately if employee: (1) assumes false identity/forged documents; (2) commits error causing substantial loss (report to MOHRE within 7 days); (3) violates safety instructions (after written warning); (4) fails basic duties (after written warning + investigation); (5) discloses employer secrets causing damage; (6) intoxication/drugs during work; (7) assault on employer/manager/colleagues; (8) absent without valid reason >20 non-consecutive days or >7 consecutive days/year (after written warning at 10/5 days).

**Even for Article 44:** Document thoroughly, conduct investigation, give employee opportunity to respond, issue in writing citing specific ground.

### By Employee — Resignation
**With notice (Article 43):** Written notice per contract (30-90 days). Must continue working during notice. Full gratuity (no reduction under new law).
**Without notice (Article 45):** Allowed if employer fails obligations, assaults employee, or dangerous working conditions.

### During Probation
Employer: 14 days' notice. Employee: 14 days if staying in UAE; 1 month if leaving.

### Arbitrary Dismissal (Article 47)
Termination for reasons unrelated to work = arbitrary. Court may award up to 3 months' remuneration (on top of notice pay and gratuity). Employee must file MOHRE complaint first.

### Step-by-Step Termination Process
1. Document reason (legitimate, non-arbitrary)
2. Issue written termination notice with last working day
3. Handle notice period (working or garden leave)
4. Calculate final settlement: outstanding salary + unused annual leave (basic salary) + gratuity + repatriation ticket + contractual entitlements
5. Pay within 14 days of last working day
6. Cancel visa through MOHRE/GDRFA within 30 days
7. Employee gets 30-day grace period after cancellation

## Emiratisation and Nafis

### Who Must Comply
Private sector companies with **50+ employees** in 14 designated economic activities, registered with MOHRE (mainland). **Exempt:** <50 employees, free zone companies (generally — evolving), DIFC/ADGM.

### Targets
2% annual increase in Emirati employees in skilled roles (secondary school certificate or higher).

| Year-end | Target rate (skilled jobs, 50+ employees) |
|---|---|
| 31 Dec 2024 | 6% |
| 31 Dec 2025 | 8% |
| **31 Dec 2026** | **10%** |

Verified 2026-04-25. Re-check mohre.gov.ae before advising any client on year-specific compliance.

### Emirati Minimum Wage (Private Sector)
**AED 6,000/month effective 1 January 2026.** Applies to UAE nationals in the private sector. Set by MOHRE Cabinet decision. Verify current rate before quoting in any offer letter.

### Penalties
Monthly financial contributions per missing Emirati employee — currently AED 9,000/month per missing position (AED 108,000/year). 2024 amendments to Federal Decree-Law 33/2021 also raised general permit/dues fines to **AED 100,000–AED 1,000,000** for hiring without valid permits or failing to settle dues. **Always web search for current amounts before advising.**

### For Companies Under 50 Employees
Not subject to mandatory quotas. Can access Nafis subsidies if hiring Emiratis. May gain preferential treatment in procurement. Future expansion to smaller companies is possible.

### Nafis Benefits
Salary top-up from government, child allowance, GPSSA pension (employer ~12.5%, employee ~5%), unemployment insurance, funded training.

### What NOT To Do
Fake Emiratisation (paper hires), minimum wage gaming, quick-hire-quick-fire cycles around audit periods. MOHRE actively detects and penalizes these.

## Part-Time, Flexible, and Freelancer Work

**Part-time:** Working for one or more employers for specified hours/days. Leave and gratuity pro-rated. Written contract required.

**Freelancer permit:** Not an employment relationship. No employer-employee obligations. No gratuity, no WPS. Available in free zones and through MOHRE. See visas-immigration reference for details.

## Anti-Discrimination and Equal Pay

**Article 4:** Prohibits discrimination based on race, colour, sex, religion, national origin, social origin, or disability in all employment practices. Women entitled to same pay for same/equal value work. Harassment (sexual, bullying, verbal/physical/psychological violence) prohibited — can trigger disciplinary action, termination, criminal liability.

## DIFC and ADGM

Separate employment laws. **DIFC:** DIFC Employment Law (Law 2/2019). **ADGM:** ADGM Employment Regulations 2019. Different termination rules, gratuity calculations, leave entitlements. Disputes handled by DIFC/ADGM Courts, not MOHRE. Web search difc.ae or adgm.com for current regulations.

## Escalation

🟢 **Green:** Standard hiring, contract requirements, leave calculations, working hours, basic gratuity, WPS compliance, general MOHRE procedures.
🟡 **Amber:** Non-compete enforcement, complex termination, Emiratisation penalty disputes, restructuring/redundancies (5+ employees), executive agreements, unfair termination claims.
🔴 **Red:** Active MOHRE complaints/labour court proceedings, collective redundancies, whistleblower claims, discrimination/harassment investigations, DIFC/ADGM employment disputes, criminal matters from employment.
