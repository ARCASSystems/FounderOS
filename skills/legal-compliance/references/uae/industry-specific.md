## Cross-Industry Requirements

Every business in the UAE needs (regardless of industry):
1. Trade license with correct activity code (see company-formation reference)
2. VAT registration if above threshold (see tax-vat reference)
3. Corporate tax registration (see tax-vat reference)
4. Employment compliance for employees (see employment-mohre reference)
5. Data protection compliance if collecting personal data (see data-protection reference)

Industry-specific regulations are **in addition to** these.

## Events & Entertainment

**Key regulators:** DTCM (Dubai), DCT (Abu Dhabi), Dubai Municipality, Dubai Police, Civil Defence, Dubai Sports Council.
Web search: visitdubai.com, dubaipolice.gov.ae

### When You Need a Permit
Any public/semi-public event: conferences, exhibitions, concerts, product launches, corporate events, festivals, pop-ups. Private internal team events generally don't need DTCM permit but may need venue approvals.

### Permit Process (Dubai — DTCM)
Apply through DTCM (online) → submit event details → obtain NOCs from relevant authorities (Police, Civil Defence, Municipality, Sports Council as needed) → approval → post-event reporting (some permits).

**Timeline:** Apply minimum **2-4 weeks** before (6-8 weeks for large events).

### Key Compliance
- Crowd safety/security (scales with attendance)
- Fire safety (Civil Defence clearance)
- Food safety (if serving food — Municipality food permit)
- Alcohol (venue must have liquor license; temporary possible)
- Content (cannot violate UAE public morals)
- Insurance (public liability recommended/sometimes required)
- Music/entertainment: may need National Media Council/MRO clearance + copyright licensing

## Education & Training

**Key regulators:** KHDA (Dubai), ADEK (Abu Dhabi), MOE (Federal), CAA (accreditation).
Web search: khda.gov.ae, adek.gov.ae, moe.gov.ae

### Training Institutes
Need trade license with education/training activity + KHDA training institute permit (Dubai). Requirements: qualified trainers (minimum bachelor's + experience), suitable premises, curriculum approval for certain subjects, Civil Defence clearance, annual renewal and inspection.

### Online Training / E-Learning
Evolving regulations. Free zone approval may be needed. Accredited courses need CAA recognition. Content regulations apply. Web search for current rules.

### Tutoring
Dubai: KHDA registration required. Individual tutors need permit. Operating without = penalties.

### Key Distinction
- **KHDA approval** = permitted to operate as training institute
- **Accreditation** (CAA/international bodies) = courses recognized for academic credit
- Most corporate training needs KHDA approval only, not accreditation

## Technology & SaaS

**Key regulators:** TDRA (telecom/digital), Central Bank (fintech), VARA (Dubai crypto), FSRA/ADGM, DFSA/DIFC.
Web search: tdra.gov.ae, centralbank.ae

### General Tech (Software, SaaS, IT Services)
**No special industry permits** beyond trade license with appropriate tech activity code. Standard compliance (tax, employment, data protection) applies.

### Fintech and Payments
If your tech touches money, you likely need regulatory approval:

| Activity | Regulator |
|---|---|
| Payment processing | Central Bank (Retail Payment Services) |
| E-wallets / stored value | Central Bank |
| P2P lending | Central Bank / DFSA / FSRA |
| Robo-advisory | SCA / DFSA / FSRA |
| Crowdfunding | SCA / DFSA / FSRA |
| Insurtech | Insurance Authority / DFSA / FSRA |

**Sandboxes:** DIFC Innovation Testing License, ADGM RegLab, Central Bank Sandbox — test before full licensing.

### Virtual Assets / Crypto
**Dubai (VARA):** All virtual asset service providers must be VARA-licensed. Covers exchanges, brokers, custodians, lending, management, issuance. Does not apply to DIFC or ADGM.
**ADGM (FSRA):** First comprehensive virtual asset framework. Institutional-grade.
**DIFC (DFSA):** Crypto token regime, more limited scope.
Web search for current status — fastest-moving area of UAE law.

### AI
No specific licensing yet. AI ethics guidelines published but not mandatory for private sector. Sector-specific AI rules may apply (healthcare, financial). Web search for developments.

## Consulting & Professional Services

**Lightest-regulated sector.** Most consulting needs only a trade license with correct activity code — no special permits.

### When Consulting Becomes Regulated

| If You Consult On... | You May Need... |
|---|---|
| Financial/investment advice | SCA/DFSA/FSRA license |
| Legal advice | Registered legal practitioner |
| Tax advice (as agent) | FTA tax agent registration |
| Audit/assurance | Ministry of Economy registration |
| Healthcare advisory | DHA/DOH approval |
| Real estate advisory | RERA broker license |
| Immigration consultancy | GDRFA/ICP license |

**Rule:** Strategic/operational business advice = fine with consultancy license. Regulated field advice = check for specific registration.

### For Typical Service Business (15-35 employees)
Free zone professional license (or mainland for government clients), visas matching team size, service agreements with IP/confidentiality clauses, corporate tax + VAT if threshold, MOHRE compliance, professional indemnity insurance (recommended).

## E-Commerce

**Key law:** Federal Decree-Law No. 15/2020 (Consumer Protection), Cabinet Decision 81/2021 (E-commerce regs).
Web search: det.gov.ae, consumerrights.ae

### Licensing
**E-Trader (DET Dubai):** For individuals selling online. Simplified, lower cost, linked to Emirates ID. Cannot hire or sponsor visas.
**Full e-commerce license:** For companies with employees, warehouse, significant volume.
**Free zone:** Several zones offer e-commerce packages (DMCC, CommerCity).

### Mandatory Disclosures
Must clearly display: legal identity (name, license, address), contact details, accurate product descriptions, total price including taxes and delivery, payment methods, delivery terms, return/refund policy, privacy policy.

### Consumer Rights
Return within specified period (varies by product — web search), full transparency on pricing, cancel before delivery for full refund, defective goods: replace/repair/refund.

### Payment and Social Media
PCI DSS compliance for card payments. Cash on delivery common in UAE. Payment gateway must be UAE-licensed/recognized. Social media selling (Instagram, WhatsApp, TikTok) still requires license (E-Trader minimum) + all consumer protection rules + advertising disclosure for influencer partnerships.

## Food & Beverage

**Key regulators:** Dubai Municipality (Food Safety), ADAFSA (Abu Dhabi), ESMA (federal standards), DHA (health cards).
Web search: dm.gov.ae, esma.gov.ae

### Restaurant / Café Requirements
Trade license + food permit (Municipality) + premises inspection + food handler health cards (all food-handling staff) + Civil Defence clearance + environmental health approval.

### Cloud/Dark Kitchens
Same food safety as restaurants. No dine-in but kitchen must meet standards.

### Food Trading / Import
Import permit from Municipality, product registration for new products, ESMA conformity, halal certification for meat.

### Key Compliance
- **HACCP** increasingly required for larger operations
- Regular unannounced inspections
- **Labelling (ESMA):** product name, ingredients, allergens, nutrition, dates, origin, storage, net weight, barcode, halal marking, Arabic + English
- **Halal:** All meat must be halal-certified. Strict enforcement.
- **Alcohol:** Requires liquor license. Only certain venue types qualify. 21+ age verification.

### Violations
Warnings, fines, temporary closure, permanent closure for serious violations.

## Healthcare

**Key regulators:** DHA (Dubai), DOH (Abu Dhabi), MOHAP (Federal + Northern Emirates), DHCC (Healthcare City).
Web search: dha.gov.ae, doh.gov.ae, mohap.gov.ae

### Clinics and Medical Centres
Trade license + healthcare facility license + professional licensing for all practitioners + premises inspection + medical director (licensed physician) + clinical governance + mandatory professional liability insurance.

### Professional Licensing
All healthcare professionals individually licensed. Credentials verified through dataflow primary source verification. License tied to employing facility. CME requirements. Malpractice insurance required. Practicing without license = criminal offense.

### Health Tech
Apps providing medical advice: may need DHA/DOH registration. Telemedicine: specific regulatory approval. Health data: highest protection category. Medical devices (including software as medical device): registration required.

### Advertising
Must be approved by regulatory authority. Cannot make unsubstantiated claims or guarantee outcomes. Social media regulated. Before-and-after photos have specific rules.

### Always Amber or Red
Healthcare regulation is complex with personal liability. **Always recommend professional regulatory advice** for licensing, facility setup, or compliance questions.
