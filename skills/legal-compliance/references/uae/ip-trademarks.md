| Federal Law No. 11/2021 | Industrial Property (patents, designs, trade secrets) |

## Trademarks

### What Can Be Trademarked
Words, names, letters, numbers, shapes, colours, holograms, sounds, smells, or any combination — if distinctive and capable of identifying goods/services.

### Registration Process (6 Steps)
1. **Search** — Ministry of Economy portal (moec.gov.ae) for conflicts
2. **Application** — file through Ministry e-services. Specify mark, goods/services classes (Nice Classification), applicant details
3. **Examination** — distinctiveness and conflict check
4. **Publication** — Trademarks Bulletin for 30 days (opposition period)
5. **Registration certificate** — if no opposition (or resolved)
6. **Duration:** 10 years from filing, renewable indefinitely for 10-year periods

### Nice Classification — Common Classes

| Class | Covers | Typical SME Use |
|---|---|---|
| 35 | Advertising, business management, consulting | Consulting firms, agencies |
| 41 | Education, training, entertainment | Training companies, events, coaching |
| 42 | Scientific/tech services, software, IT consulting | Tech companies, SaaS |
| 9 | Software, apps, downloadable content | Software products, apps |
| 36 | Financial affairs, real estate | Financial services, property |
| 43 | Food/drink services, accommodation | Restaurants, hotels |

**Tip:** File in every class where you operate AND plan to expand within 5 years.

### Cost and Timeline

| Item | Approximate (AED) |
|---|---|
| Filing per class | 2,000-3,000 |
| Agent fees | 2,000-5,000 |
| Publication fee | 500-1,000 |
| Registration certificate | 500-1,000 |
| **Total per class** | **5,000-10,000** |
| Renewal (10 years) | 3,000-5,000/class |

**Timeline:** Filing to examination: 1-3 months. Examination to publication: 1-2 months. Publication: 30 days. Certificate: 1-2 months. **Total: 4-8 months.**

### Renewal
Every 10 years. Apply within last year or within 6 months after expiry (late fee).

### GCC and International
UAE marks protect only in UAE. GCC: register separately per country (no unified system yet). International: **Madrid Protocol** (UAE member) — one application through WIPO designating multiple countries.

### Common Rejection Reasons
Too descriptive ("Best Consulting"), confusingly similar to existing mark, contains national flags/symbols/government emblems, generic terms.

## Copyright

### Automatic Protection
Arises upon creation — no registration required. Protects: literary, artistic, music, film, software, databases, architectural, photographs — any original creative expression.

### Registration (Optional but Recommended)
Through Ministry of Economy. Creates presumption of ownership (useful in disputes). Recommended for high-value works.

### Duration
Lifetime of author + 50 years. Works by legal entities: 50 years from first publication. Software: 50 years from creation/first publication.

### Rights
**Economic:** Reproduction, distribution, public performance, broadcasting, translation, adaptation. Assignable/licensable.
**Moral:** Attribution + integrity. Cannot be waived under UAE law but can be agreed not to be exercised.

## Patents and Industrial Designs

**Patents:** Ministry of Economy. 20-year protection. Must be novel, inventive, industrially applicable. UAE is PCT member — international filing available.

**Industrial Designs:** Ornamental/aesthetic aspect. 10 years (5+5 renewal). Ministry registration.

## Trade Secrets

Protected under Federal Law 11/2021 if: (1) secret (not generally known), (2) has commercial value because secret, (3) holder took reasonable steps to keep it secret.

**Practical protection matters as much as legal:** NDAs with employees/contractors, access controls (need-to-know), documented confidentiality policies, technical security measures, mark documents as confidential.

## IP Ownership Defaults

| Scenario | Default Owner |
|---|---|
| Employee creates work as part of job | Employer (Decree-Law 38/2021) |
| Contractor creates work for client | **Contractor retains** |
| Joint work | Co-owned — each needs consent of others |
| Commissioned work | Commissioner has limited usage rights; creator retains copyright |

**Always use contracts.** Default rules rarely match expectations. See contracts-commercial reference for clause guidance.

### IP Co-Ownership
Each co-owner can use but cannot license/assign without consent. Revenue sharing must be contractual. Deadlock resolution essential. Consider separate co-ownership agreement.

## IP Holding Structures

### Why Separate Entity?
Tax efficiency (licensing royalties), asset protection, licensing flexibility, valuation/exit readiness.

### Common UAE Structures

| Structure | Suitable For |
|---|---|
| ADGM entity | International licensing, common law governance |
| JAFZA/RAK offshore | Cost-effective, international invoicing |
| DIFC entity | Financial/tech IP, investor-facing |
| Free zone operating company | Simple — hold and operate in same entity |

**Tax note:** Royalty payments between related entities must be arm's length (transfer pricing). See tax-vat reference.

## DIFC IP Regime

DIFC Law 4/2019 operates separately from federal IP law. Applies to IP created, owned, or exploited within DIFC. Common law principles. DIFC Courts enforcement. Registration through DIFC IP Office. Web search difc.ae for current procedures.
