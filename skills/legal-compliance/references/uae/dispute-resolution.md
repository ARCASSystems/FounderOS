# UAE Dispute Resolution

**Last verified: 2026-04-25.** Material change: **MOHRE now has binding decision authority for employment disputes ≤AED 50,000** (2024 amendments to Federal Decree-Law 33/2021, in full effect through 2026). For employment disputes under that threshold, MOHRE is the first and binding forum, not the courts. Above AED 50K, court route still applies. No other material 2025-2026 dispute-resolution amendments audited at this refresh — verify dc.gov.ae, difccourts.ae, diac.ae for current fees and procedural updates.

## Court System

| Court | Jurisdiction | Language | System |
|---|---|---|---|
| UAE Federal Courts | Federal disputes, appeals from non-judicial emirates | Arabic | Civil law |
| Dubai Courts | Disputes in Dubai | Arabic | Civil law |
| Abu Dhabi Courts | Disputes in Abu Dhabi | Arabic | Civil law |
| DIFC Courts | DIFC parties, opt-in jurisdiction | English | Common law |
| ADGM Courts | ADGM parties, opt-in jurisdiction | English | Common law |

**Hierarchy (Dubai example):** Court of First Instance → Court of Appeal → Court of Cassation (points of law only).

## Labour Disputes

**Most common dispute type for SME founders.**

### Process
1. **Internal resolution** — try to resolve directly (documentation matters)
2. **MOHRE complaint** — employee files through MOHRE app/service centre. MOHRE attempts conciliation within **14 days**
3. **Labour Court referral** — if conciliation fails, case referred to Labour Court (part of Court of First Instance)
4. **Hearing** — court examines evidence, issues judgment
5. **Appeal** — either party can appeal

### Key Points
- MOHRE conciliation is **mandatory** before court
- Labour cases typically faster than commercial
- Courts tend to be **employee-protective**
- Judgments cover: gratuity, notice pay, arbitrary dismissal compensation, outstanding salary, leave balance
- Legal representation recommended but not required

**Timeline:** MOHRE: 14 days. Labour Court: 2-6 months. Appeal: 3-6 months additional.

## Commercial Disputes

**If contract has arbitration clause:** Must go to arbitration (court will refuse). **If no clause:** UAE courts have jurisdiction.

### UAE Court Process
1. File claim (court fees: typically 1-5% of claim, capped)
2. Service of process
3. Exchange of memoranda (written submissions)
4. Hearing (judge reviews submissions and evidence)
5. Judgment
6. Enforcement

**Timeline:** First Instance: 6-18 months. Appeal: 6-12 months. Cassation: 6-12 months.

## Arbitration

### Main Centres

| Centre | Based In | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| **DIAC** | Dubai | Dubai-seated commercial disputes |
| **DIFC-LCIA** | DIFC | High-value, international |
| **ADCCAC** | Abu Dhabi | Abu Dhabi-seated |
| **ICC** | Paris (UAE-seated) | International |

**Law:** Federal Law No. 6/2018 (based on UNCITRAL Model Law).

**Advantages:** Confidential, party-appointed arbitrators, faster than courts (usually), internationally enforceable (New York Convention), English proceedings possible.

**Disadvantages:** Expensive (arbitrator + institutional + legal fees), limited appeal, no injunctive power without court assistance.

**Cost:** AED 50,000-500,000+ depending on complexity and claim value. Small claims often not cost-effective in arbitration.

## Small Claims

**Dubai Small Claims Tribunal:** Up to AED 500,000 (companies) / AED 50,000 (individuals). Simplified, faster, limited appeal. No lawyer required (recommended for complex).

**DIFC Small Claims Tribunal:** Up to USD 500,000. English language. Simplified and expedited.

## Debt Collection

### For Unpaid Invoices
1. **Formal demand letter** (registered mail — creates evidence)
2. **Payment order** (summary process for undisputed debts)
3. **Full court proceedings** (if disputed)
4. **Execution** — apply to Execution Court to enforce judgment

**Bounced cheques:** Criminal offense (though recent amendments have somewhat decriminalized). Bounced cheque serves as evidence of debt and expedites enforcement.

## DIFC Courts Special Jurisdiction

Accept jurisdiction for: (1) cases involving a DIFC entity, (2) opt-in by both parties (even if neither is DIFC), (3) enforcement of foreign judgments and arbitral awards ("conduit jurisdiction").

**Why opt in:** English, common law, professional specialized judges, efficient, enforceable in Dubai/UAE.

## Enforcement

**UAE judgments:** Execution Court can attach assets, freeze accounts, impose travel bans. 3-12 months depending on complexity.

**Foreign judgments:** Recognized under bilateral treaties or reciprocity. GCC judgments enforceable under GCC Convention.

**Arbitral awards:** Domestic: enforce through Court of First Instance. International: New York Convention (UAE signatory). DIFC-seated: through DIFC Courts → ratified in Dubai Courts.

| Route | Cost (AED) | Timeline |

Rough ranges. Actual depends on claim value, complexity, lawyer fees, duration. Get cost estimate from lawyer before proceeding.
